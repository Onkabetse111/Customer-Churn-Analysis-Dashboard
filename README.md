# Customer-Churn-Analysis-Dashboard
This project presents a Customer Churn Analysis Dashboard built in Microsoft Excel to analyze customer behavior, identify key drivers of churn, and quantify the financial impact of customer attrition.

The dashboard leverages pivot tables, calculated columns, and interactive visualizations to transform raw data into actionable business insights.

## 🎯 Business Problem

Customer churn poses a significant challenge to businesses, leading to loss of revenue and reduced customer lifetime value. Without understanding why customers leave, organizations struggle to implement effective retention strategies.

This project aims to answer key business questions:

- Which customers are most likely to churn?
- What factors (age, geography, product usage, balance) drive churn?
- How much revenue is being lost due to customer attrition?
- Which customer segments should be prioritized for retention?

---

## 📊 Key Insights

- Customer churn rate is 20.38%, meaning approximately 1 in 5 customers are leaving.
- Germany has the highest churn (~32%), indicating region-specific challenges.
- Female customers churn more (~25%) than males (~16%).
- Customers with 2 products have the lowest churn, while single-product users churn more.
- High-balance customers show higher churn, posing a significant financial risk.
- Customers aged 50+ have the highest churn (~45%).
- Revenue loss from churn exceeds $185M, highlighting the financial impact.

---

## 🧠 Business Recommendations

- Implement cross-selling strategies to increase product adoption and improve customer engagement.
- Focus retention efforts on high-churn regions (e.g., Germany) with targeted campaigns.
- Develop personalized retention programs for high-value customers to reduce financial loss.
- Improve customer onboarding and engagement strategies to retain low-product users.
- Introduce age-specific retention initiatives, especially for older customer segments.
